Understanding GLP-1 Therapy

What are GLP-1 medications and how do they work?

GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) receptor agonists are a class of medications that have shown remarkable effectiveness for weight management. They work by mimicking a hormone your body naturally produces after eating.

GLP-1 medications work through several key mechanisms:

  • Appetite regulation — Signal the brain to reduce hunger and increase feelings of fullness
  • Gastric slowing — Slow the rate at which food leaves the stomach, extending satiety
  • Insulin response — Stimulate insulin release in response to blood sugar levels
  • Blood sugar balance — Help maintain healthy glucose levels throughout the day

Clinical studies have associated GLP-1 therapy with significant body weight reduction. Individual results vary based on dose, adherence, diet, and activity level. A physician evaluation is required — not everyone qualifies.

Semaglutide vs. Tirzepatide

Which GLP-1 program is right for you?

Compounded Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist. It targets one receptor pathway to regulate appetite and blood sugar. It has the longest clinical track record of any GLP-1 therapy and is an excellent starting point, beginning at $189/mo.

Compounded Tirzepatide is a dual GIP and GLP-1 receptor agonist. It targets two pathways simultaneously. Clinical studies have associated tirzepatide with greater average weight reduction compared to semaglutide alone. Starting at $249/mo.

Your board-certified physician will recommend the most appropriate program based on your health history and goals. Both programs include B12 and are dispensed as injectable vials by our licensed 503B pharmacy partner.

Wellness & Peptide Programs

Beyond GLP-1 — peptide therapy for complete wellness

Peptides are short chains of amino acids — the building blocks of proteins — that act as signaling molecules in the body.
